# Tetsubin 101 Research and Article Implementation Plan

> **For agentic workers:** REQUIRED SUB-SKILL: Use superpowers:subagent-driven-development (recommended) or superpowers:executing-plans to implement this plan task-by-task. Steps use checkbox (`- [ ]`) syntax for tracking.

**Goal:** Produce a source-audited research dossier and outline, obtain user approval, then publish a research-led “Tetsubin 101” draft for advanced gongfu tea practitioners.

**Architecture:** Separate evidence collection, editorial synthesis, visual-rights tracking, and publication into focused Markdown artifacts. The research dossier is the source of truth; the outline maps accepted claims into a narrative; the tea post contains only claims supported by the dossier. Drafting and publication remain blocked until the user approves the completed research stage.

**Tech Stack:** Markdown, Astro content collections, repository title/SEO rules, authoritative web and academic research, shell-based content checks, and `npm run build`.

## Global Constraints

- Work only on the `codex/tetsubin-101` branch unless the user directs otherwise.
- Use an object-first field-guide structure for advanced gongfu practitioners.
- Do not invent ownership, experiments, sensory results, or first-person tetsubin experience.
- Classify water claims as demonstrated, plausible, reported, or unsupported.
- Do not make medical, nutritional-treatment, or iron-deficiency claims.
- Prefer Japanese institutional and craft sources for history and construction, and peer-reviewed or authoritative technical sources for chemistry and safety.
- Identify workshop and retail claims as trade sources rather than neutral evidence.
- Record conflicts between credible sources instead of silently resolving them.
- Use real, rights-cleared photography whenever an image functions as evidence.
- The user chooses the hero image; do not select, generate, or add a final hero image.
- Follow `.cursor/rules/post-titles.mdc`, `.cursor/rules/seo.mdc`, and repository tea-post conventions.
- Do not add new packages or change site architecture.

- [ ] **Step 3: Collect construction sources from established workshops**

Use multiple established workshops rather than relying on one production account. Record where methods differ, including mold material, mold reuse, inner-core preparation, surface patterning, oxidation treatment, lacquer or other exterior finishing, handle construction, and lid production.

```text
鉄瓶 溶出鉄 水質 研究

```

For each technical source, record test material, water composition, pH, heating duration, temperature, measured iron species when reported, and whether the experiment used an actual unlined tetsubin or another cast-iron vessel. Evidence from other cast-iron cookware may support a mechanism but cannot directly prove a tetsubin-specific sensory claim.

## Task 2: Research the Object, History, and Construction

**Files:**
- Modify: `docs/research/tetsubin-101-research.md`

**Interfaces:**
- Consumes: Source IDs and evidence schema from Task 1.
- Produces: Article-safe findings for the first four article sections and terminology for the anatomy visual.

- [ ] **Step 1: Resolve the core definition**

Document the defining features of a tetsubin and the terminology needed to distinguish it from an enameled cast-iron teapot, kama, chagama, and other nearby vessel categories. Record whether historical and modern sources use these boundaries consistently.

- [ ] **Step 2: Build a restrained historical timeline**

Research the emergence of tetsubin as a distinct kettle, its relationship to earlier Japanese iron kettles, the development of major production centers, and the transition between utilitarian, sencha-associated, decorative, and collectible uses.

Explicitly test common “400-year history” language: separate the age of a regional ironworking tradition from the documented age of tetsubin as a vessel form.

- [ ] **Step 3: Document anatomy and production**

Describe the function and construction of the body, spout, lid, knob, handle, handle attachments, inner core, bottom, and interior surface. Preserve verified Japanese terms alongside the clearest English terms.

Map the production sequence from pattern and molds through casting, core removal, finishing, oxidation treatment, exterior coloring or lacquering, handle fitting, and inspection. Note variants rather than forcing all workshops into one sequence.

- [ ] **Step 4: Research how workmanship and condition are read**

Record evidence-supported observations about wall thickness, weight, casting seams, surface pattern, spout finish, handle movement, lid construction, interior condition, repairs, leakage, active flaking corrosion, stable oxide, and mineral scale.

Keep age authentication and signature attribution limited to broad warning signs; a comprehensive maker or signature catalogue is out of scope.

- [ ] **Step 5: Add claims and conflicts to the matrix**

Every article-safe statement about origin, dates, regional traditions, or production must receive a claim ID, classification, confidence level, supporting source IDs, and any conflicting account.

- [ ] **Step 6: Run the scope and language check**

Run:

```bash

```

Expected: every absolute term is either directly justified by strong evidence, quoted as a disputed claim, or revised into appropriately qualified language.

## Task 3: Research Water, Gongfu Use, Care, and Buying

**Files:**
- Modify: `docs/research/tetsubin-101-research.md`

**Interfaces:**
- Consumes: Technical corpus and claim-matrix schema from Task 1.
- Produces: Evidence-graded findings for the water, use, care, limits, and buying sections.

- [ ] **Step 1: Separate measurable variables from sensory conclusions**

For every water study or technical source, extract the starting water composition, pH, heating duration, temperature, iron material, measured output, and experimental limitations. Record direct findings separately from extrapolations to tea extraction.

Evaluate claims about dissolved iron, pH, alkalinity, hardness, chlorine, heat retention, and perceived sweetness individually. Do not treat “softer water” as a technical conclusion unless the source measured and defined it.

- [ ] **Step 2: Map evidence to gongfu brewing**

Explain which established water variables can affect extraction and taste, then identify whether tetsubin-specific evidence connects the kettle to those variables. Tea-family pairings and flavor descriptions belong in `reported` unless controlled evidence supports more.

- [ ] **Step 3: Research safe preparation and daily operation**

Document first-use preparation, appropriate fill level, dry-boiling risk, suitable and unsuitable heat sources, pouring and handle safety, emptying, residual-heat drying, storage, and precautions around sudden temperature change.

Where maker instructions differ, record the difference and prefer advice that is both widely supported and conservative.

- [ ] **Step 4: Distinguish rust, stable oxide, and scale**

Define the visible and functional difference between stable interior oxidation, loose or active red corrosion, and mineral scale. Research when continued use, gentle cleaning, professional repair, or rejection is justified. Avoid declaring a kettle safe from photographs alone.

- [ ] **Step 5: Build the compact buying framework**

Organize buying guidance around intended use, capacity, filled weight, wall thickness, spout and pour, handle stability, lid, interior condition, leakage, repairs, provenance, and price-driving craft factors. Use broad quality categories without current prices, ranked products, or named recommendations.

- [ ] **Step 6: Audit unsupported and medical claims**

Run:

```bash
rg -n -i 'healthy|health benefit|iron deficiency|anemia|detox|removes chlorine|sweetens|softens water|best tea' docs/research/tetsubin-101-research.md
```

Expected: matches appear only in evidence-qualified analysis or the disputed-claims section; none is presented as an unqualified benefit.

- [ ] **Step 7: Commit practical and scientific findings**

```bash

git commit -m "docs: research tetsubin water and use"
```
