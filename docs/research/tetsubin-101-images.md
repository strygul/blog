## Rights and Production Gates

- Only rows with an affirmative reusable-rights approval may advance to download, edit, or publication. `candidate-permission-required` remains blocked until the exact written grant or copyright assignment is linked in the rights record. `rejected-rights-unclear` is reference-only and must never enter production.
- The Smithsonian object’s active “Usage Conditions Apply” status controls this plan. Its generic CC0 text and a generic CC0 deed do not establish rights for FSC-M-30a-b; use requires a written grant from the linked Image Services and Permissions route. The Wikimedia decision remains valid and requires creator credit, a license link, a change note for every crop, and ShareAlike treatment for adaptations.
- Smithsonian, Iwachu, Oigen, and Tea Masters are direct-source leads, not currently publishable assets. Their photographs may function as evidence only after written permission covers the exact files and planned editorial treatment.
- Search-engine thumbnails, retailer listings, auction photographs, Pinterest reposts, and unattributed social posts were excluded. They are not substitute source pages and cannot be used to illustrate condition, authenticity, or construction.
- The filenames above are reservations only. No image has been downloaded, embedded, generated, or added to the repository.
- Rights approval does not establish factual adequacy. Captions must remain within the dossier’s evidence limits, and no photograph may diagnose an interior finish, corrosion chemistry, structural safety, or authenticity by color or texture alone.

## Anatomy Plate Specification

The plate is a production brief only. Do not create it until the research review is approved. Create a restrained original left-facing side elevation from the dossier’s verified terminology and construction findings, with leader lines around the silhouette, plus a separate simplified vertical cutaway inset. Do not trace, reproduce, or use a museum photograph as a visual base. The inset must be explicitly labeled as a casting-process schematic and must not imply that every workshop uses the same mold recipe.

| Label | Verified factual source | Intended view | Distinction that must remain visible |
|---|---|---|---|
| Body — *dō* / *hontai* (胴 / 本体) | Dossier “Anatomy and Construction,” SRC-009 | Side elevation and cutaway | The water-bearing cast shell; keep it distinct from the base mold and the temporary core |
| Bottom/base — *shiri* (尻) | SRC-009 | Side elevation and cutaway | Heat-contact area; show the documented separate bottom-mold region without implying that the finished base is a detachable part |
| Mouth — *kuchi* (口), and shoulder | Dossier “Anatomy and Construction,” supported by SRC-005 and SRC-009 | Side elevation | The mouth receives the lid; the shoulders carry the handle lugs |
| Spout — *sosogiguchi* (注ぎ口) | SRC-009 | Side elevation and cutaway | The spout is modeled with a small core in the documented traditional sequence, then casts with the body; do not draw it as a screwed-on tube |
| Handle lugs — *kan-tsuki* (鐶付) | SRC-005; SRC-009 | Side elevation, with one enlarged attachment inset if space permits | Show the pair cast into the shoulders and the separate handle seated through them |
| Overhead handle — *tsuru* (鉉 / 弦) | SRC-005; SRC-014; SRC-023 | Side elevation | Show it as a separately fitted overhead handle; avoid implying one universal metal, solid/hollow construction, or folding behavior |
| Lid — *futa* (蓋) | SRC-009; SRC-012; SRC-014 | Side elevation and cutaway | Show it as a separate fitted element; body and lid materials need not match |
| Knob — *tsumami* (つまみ) | SRC-009 | Side elevation | Keep the knob distinct from the lid and avoid turning its shape into an authenticity test |
| Internal core — *nakago* (中子) | SRC-009; SRC-010; SRC-023 | Cutaway only, in a contrasting dashed or pale tone | Label “temporary mold core—broken out after casting.” The visible gap to the outer mold establishes the wall and hollow interior; the core is not present in a finished kettle |

The plate must also carry three concise cautions: “schematic; not to scale,” “documented traditional sequence—workshop methods vary,” and “interior color does not identify oxide, lacquer, resin, enamel, deposits, or corrosion.” Do not add unverified names for rim profiles, vents, decorative motifs, feet, seams, filters, or interior layers.

## Comparison and Sequence Assembly Notes

- IMG-005 and IMG-006 are a single editorial comparison but retain separate rights decisions. Both source photographs require written permission, so the assembled comparison remains blocked until both grants are recorded.
- The comparison should use matched scale, angle, and interior crops where the source files permit it. Do not use exterior color, a basket infuser, or the English retail word “tetsubin” as the deciding test.
