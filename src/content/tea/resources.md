### Links
<a href="https://www.teatechnique.org/">www.teatechnique.org</a> – <i>An Introduction to the Art & Science of Chinese 
Tea Ceremony</i> by Jason M. Cohen is an in-depth e-book exploring Chinese tea culture from multiple angles. It takes 
you on a journey from the origins and history of tea all the way to the chemical makeup of various zisha clays. A
must-read for anyone who takes their tea hobby seriously. The full version of the book is available on a subscription 
basis, however, some chapters of it are in open access.

<a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/teapot2">Early Teapots II</a> – a Facebook group dedicated to discussions 
surrounding the book <i>Early Teapots Encyclopedia Vol. II</i>, moderated by its author, Chi Lin Lu. The group holds a 
tremendous amount of information on antique Yixing teapots (especially F1), much of which is difficult to find 
elsewhere—provided you’re willing to dig through hundreds of posts and thousands of comments. Most discussions are in 
Chinese, which adds an extra layer of challenge, but those undeterred will uncover a true well of knowledge.
