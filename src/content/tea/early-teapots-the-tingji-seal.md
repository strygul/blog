### Only One Correct Tingji Seal

Based on collected information, there is **only one correct version** of the Tingji seal [庭記]. If the Tingji seal does not match this known version, further examination is generally unnecessary.

In this context:

- **"Tingji" refers to a specific seal**,
- and **"Tingji pots"** are simply teapots that carry this seal.


### Era and Base Marks: Mainly 1960s

Tingji is **primarily associated with the 1960s**.

Two base marks [底款] are identified as the main ones that appear together with Tingji:
- **Six-character seal**
- **4:6-seal "Zhongguo Yixing"**

A pot with a **3:7-seal** has been mentioned, but it is explicitly described as still awaiting in-hand confirmation. For
practical purposes, the six-character seal and the **4:6-seal "Zhongguo Yixing"** are the **standard and reliable** base 
marks connected with Tingji at this time.

### Do Not Rely on Seals Alone

Relying solely on seals is considered insufficient because **seals can be imitated**.

Evaluation must include era-specific characteristics. To examine a Tingji pot correctly, five aspects must be considered:

#### Five Essential Elements

1. **Clay**
2. **Craft / workmanship**
3. **Shape / form**
4. **Marks / inscriptions**
5. **Kiln / firing characteristics**

A piece is considered credible only when **all five** elements align with 1960s characteristics.

### Understanding “Tingji” Within This Framework

From the information provided:

- **"Tingji" refers to a seal.**
- **"Tingji pots" are pots carrying this seal**, not a single standardized shape.
- Since multiple shapes and clays appear under the Tingji label, the unifying factor is the **Tingji seal** combined 
- with **1960s Factory No.1 characteristics**.

### Summary

- There is **one correct Tingji seal [庭記]** identified so far.
- Tingji is **from the 1960s**, with base marks mainly:
  - **Six-character seal**
  - **4:6-seal "Zhongguo Yixing"**
- A **3:7-seal** version exists only as an unverified example.
- Tingji pots may appear in multiple shapes and clays, including standard, guava/line-floating, pigeon-spout, red clay, 
- and zisha.
- Some examples bear a **Dingmei [定妹]** lid mark, said to be the nickname of **Tingmei [庭梅]**.
- Authentic evaluation must rely on the **five essential elements**: clay, craft, shape, marks, and kiln — all fitting 
- **1960s Factory No.1** characteristics.

This framework captures what is currently known about Tingji-sealed teapots and provides a basis for distinguishing 
authentic examples from the many imitations on the market.
